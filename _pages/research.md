My research lies at the intersection of **natural language processing** and **machine learning**, with a focus on building and understanding trustworthy foundation models.

## Research interests

### LLM reasoning and reinforcement learning
I am interested in how large language models acquire, represent, and improve reasoning ability, including scalable data synthesis and verifiable reasoning supervision.

### Truthfulness and interpretability
I study internal representations of LLMs to understand model truthfulness, reliability, and hallucination behavior, including representation-level signals that can support safer model outputs.

### Hallucination in vision-language models
I work on understanding and mitigating hallucination and perception bottlenecks in VLMs, particularly for structured or visually grounded reasoning settings such as chart understanding.
